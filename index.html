About

Many years to go before
my tools get retired.

  Certainly I have made most of the mistakes one can make in a trade learning the hard way, some more than once. As Jocko Willink says though, you have to own it. I surely have not made my last. There is; however, a difference between ignorance and stupidity.
   Call me naive, but it took me many years to learn the difference. There was a time when I carried a chip on my shoulder; but now, I just don't deal with it. Know the things you can change, and know when someone is wasting your time. Not to be a hypocrite, mistakes are mistakes and my scorecard has a lot. But those that would intentionally mislead or subvert another person's progress don't deserve your time. Not giving a person the tools, time and training to complete a job correctly is a recipe for disaster. Garbage in equals garbage out.
  I have learned enough and accumulated enough experience and licenses to know the difference between good intentions with mistakes and something very different from that. 10 plus years of commercial HVAC service work in buildings that ranged from strip malls up to colleges, federal buildings, city facilities, hospitals and factories has taught me the tell tail signs. With literally hundreds of service calls under my belt, I understand flexibility. But, I know what correct and not correct looks like. Life is short.
